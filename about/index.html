What is Digital Minimalism?

Digital Minimalism is an idea proposed by Cal Newport in a book with the same name. Its premise is that our lives have been improved only marginally by our modern-day digital tools. It is time to step back and critically evaluate what technology we use and why. Based on this inventory, we should intentionally incorporate only those technologies in a way that enhances our lives while discarding others.

I was inspired by this book as a software engineer to build my own philosophy for creating technology. This has led me to formalize my tenets which will undergird anything I build. Software I build should:

  • Save time and create space in the real (offline) lives of those who I serve, not take away from it.
  • Is a means to an end, not an end in and of itself.
  • Is utterly transparent in the data it collects and how it is used.
  • Does not price-gorge.
  • Does not involve online ads.
